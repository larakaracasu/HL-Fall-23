c. The current situation in China may be summarized as follows: (1) The National Government, under the leadership of the Kuomintang, is not a truly representative government. It does not enjoy the support of the Chinese people in the areas under its control, and is not in a position to extend its authority over the entire country. (2) The Chinese Communist Party, under the leadership of Mao Tse-tung, is a strong and disciplined organization, enjoying the support of the Chinese people in areas under its control. It is not in a position to extend its authority over the entire country. (3) The civil war between the National Government and the Chinese Communist Party is in a stalemate. Neither side is in a position to gain a clear-cut victory. (4) The Chinese Government is heavily dependent upon U. S. economic and military aid.
